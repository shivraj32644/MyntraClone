Kurtas are the traditional garb of choice for both men and women in many parts of southern Asia, including Sri Lanka, Pakistan, India, Bangladesh, Nepal, etc. 'Kurta', in Persian, means a shirt without a collar. Although initially worn by men, it later became popular among women's fashion trends. Women's kurtas that have a shorter length took on a new name—Kurtis.
